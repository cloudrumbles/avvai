Section 01

Design Philosophy

Every decision flows from one belief: a language learning app should feel like reading a book, not using software. The interface disappears when you're learning well. It appears — gently, contextually — only when you need help.

01

Reading is sacred

The reading surface is the most important real estate. Nothing should compete with the text. TOC, dictionary, exercises — they exist in service of reading, not alongside it.

02

One thing at a time

At any moment, the learner is doing exactly one thing: reading, answering, looking up a word, or reviewing. The UI should reflect this singular focus — never split attention across panels.

03

Context over navigation

Everything the learner needs should appear in context — definitions where you read, exercises after you learn, flashcards from what you just studied. Minimize page transitions.

04

Earned complexity

Show the minimum possible UI on first encounter. Progressive disclosure — settings, stats, word history — reveals itself as the learner invests more time.

05

Respect the source material

The Cikaram textbook is the foundation. The presentation should honor the material — prose, poetry, grammar, vocabulary — whatever each lesson contains. No gamification for its own sake. Progress tracking serves motivation, not dopamine loops.

06

One click to learn

A returning user should go from opening the app to reading Tamil in exactly one tap. Every screen between "hello" and "reading" is friction we must justify or eliminate.

Section 02

User Personas

Three archetypes represent the range of learners Avvai serves. Each has different motivations, skill levels, and session patterns.

Primary Persona
Priya — Heritage Reconnector

Age: 22 · Location: Toronto · Tamil: Conversational, can't read

Motivation: Parents spoke Tamil at home, she understands spoken Tamil but never learned to read. Wants a structured way to learn reading and writing.

Session pattern: 10-15 min daily, usually on phone during commute

Needs: Gentle script introduction, transliterations alongside Tamil text, vocabulary building, cultural context where relevant

Frustrations: Apps that assume zero Tamil knowledge, apps that feel childish, lack of structured textbook-style progression

Secondary Persona
Rajan — Academic Enthusiast

Age: 35 · Location: Chennai · Tamil: Fluent speaker, reads modern Tamil

Motivation: Can read contemporary Tamil but wants a systematic textbook-based approach to deepen fluency and expand vocabulary.

Session pattern: 30-60 min, 3-4 times per week, desktop

Needs: Deep dictionary entries, detailed analysis, ability to read without hand-holding, exercise depth over quantity

Frustrations: Too much scaffolding, not enough real Tamil content, simplistic exercises

Tertiary Persona
Kenji — Curious Outsider

Age: 28 · Location: Tokyo · Tamil: Zero knowledge

Motivation: Linguistics hobbyist, fascinated by Dravidian languages, has learned basics from other resources, wants a structured textbook-based approach.

Session pattern: 20-30 min, daily, alternating phone and desktop

Needs: Script teaching, transliteration, grammar explanations, frequent vocabulary reinforcement, word-by-word breakdowns

Frustrations: Materials that assume familiarity with Indian languages, lack of romanized pronunciation, no grammar explanations

Edge Persona
Dr. Lakshmi — Researcher

Age: 52 · Location: Madurai · Tamil: Native speaker, fluent reader

Motivation: Tamil language teacher who wants to use Avvai as an interactive companion to the Cikaram textbook for her students.

Session pattern: Occasional, desktop, explores all content

Needs: Accuracy of translations, depth of dictionary, ability to browse freely without progress gates, export/share features

Frustrations: Dumbed-down content, forced linear progression, inaccurate translations

Navigation Model

The app uses a hub-and-spoke model centered on /home. The home screen is the only "hub" — all other screens are spokes that return to it. The lesson reader is an immersive context that hides global navigation.

Key change from current: The current app has /home showing a dashboard with sidebar, then /lesson showing a list, then clicking into the reader. The new model goes: /home (resume card) → /lesson/[id] (reader). One click. The lesson browser is accessible but not mandatory.
Section 04

User Flows

Six flows cover every journey through the app. The first two (new user, returning user) are by far the most common.

Flow 1: First-Time User

New visitor → First lesson
Landing Page → Tap "Begin" → Onboarding (3 steps) → Home (first visit) → Tap "Start Lesson 1" → Lesson Reader

Flow 2: Returning User — Continue Reading

Open app → Resume reading
Home → Tap "Continue" → Lesson Reader (at last position)
This is the golden path. One tap from home to reading. The continue button is the largest, most prominent element on the home screen.

Flow 3: Returning User — Flashcard Review

Open app → Review vocabulary
Home → Tap "X cards due" → Flashcard Session → Session Complete → Home

Flow 4: Lesson Completion

Finish reading → Review → Next lesson
Lesson Reader → Reach end + complete exercises → Post-Lesson Summary → Review words OR Next lesson → Flashcards / Next Reader

Flow 5: Browse Lessons

Explore available lessons
Home → Tap "All Lessons" → Lesson Browser → Tap a lesson card → Lesson Reader

Flow 6: Word Discovery (within Reader)

Encounter unknown word → Learn it
Reading → Tap Tamil word → Dictionary Panel → Dismiss or Save → Resume Reading
Key interaction: Tapping a word should feel instant. The dictionary panel slides in without disrupting reading position. A small "save" icon adds the word to the flashcard queue without leaving the reader.

Analysis

This poem belongs to the நெய்தல் tradition, the seashore landscape that evokes the emotion of waiting and longing. The speaker addresses the moon directly, a common device in Sangam poetry.

The juxtaposition of நிலவு (moon) with பாசறை (military encampment) creates a striking contrast between the softness of moonlight and the harshness of war.

Content Ordering — The Interleaved Pattern

Exercises are not in a separate panel. They appear inline between reading sections in the scroll flow. After reading content, the learner encounters comprehension checks before continuing. Lessons from the Cikaram textbook contain a mix of section types: prose, poetry, dialogue (drama), media (images), vocabulary, and exercises — in whatever order the textbook presents them.

Example: poetry lesson (c-1-2)
Prose (Intro) → Media (Image) → Poetry → Prose (Analysis) → Vocabulary → Exercises → Lesson Complete
Example: drama lesson (c-1-3)
Prose (Intro) → Media (Image) → Dialogue (Scene 1) → Dialogue (Scene 2) → Vocabulary → Exercises (MCQ + Short + Long) → Lesson Complete

Dictionary Lookup

Not a screen — a contextual panel that appears when a Tamil word is tapped. On desktop it lives in the right margin. On mobile it slides up as a bottom sheet. It must feel instant.

TriggerTap/click any Tamil word in the reading surface
DesktopRight margin panel (320px), sticky-positioned
MobileBottom sheet (slides up, covers ~40% of screen)
DismissTap another word (replaces) · Tap outside · Swipe down (mobile) · Esc key

Dictionary Card Anatomy

  1. Tamil word — large, clear (1.4rem)
  2. Transliteration + Part of speech — "nilavu · noun" in muted text
  3. Definition — primary meaning, plus contextual usage relevant to the current lesson
  4. Example sentence — from the current lesson text, with English translation
  5. Related words — optional, shows cognates or derived forms
  6. Save button — "+ Save to flashcards" adds to SRS queue

States

Word Found

Full definition card with all fields populated. "Save to flashcards" button visible.

Loading

Word shown immediately (from click target). Skeleton placeholder for definition and examples. Should resolve in under 300ms.

Not Found

Word shown. "We don't have a definition for this word yet." No save button. Optionally: "Search online" link.

Already Saved

Same as "Word Found" but save button shows "✓ Saved" in green instead. Clicking again un-saves.

Interaction detail: When a word is tapped, the word in the text gets a subtle highlight (yellow background). The dictionary panel appears (or updates if already visible). On desktop, the panel is sticky-positioned so it stays in view even as the user scrolls. On mobile, the bottom sheet has a drag handle and can be dismissed by swiping down. The sheet doesn't block the full screen — user can still see the text above.
Screen 07

Exercises (Inline)

Exercises appear in the reading flow between content sections. They're comprehension checks that confirm the learner understood what they just read. Each exercise is self-contained — answer, get feedback, continue reading.

LocationInline in reading surface, between content sections
Visual treatmentDistinct card with warm gold background (#fdf8ec) and gold border
TypesMultiple choice · Fill in blank · Short answer · Long answer
FeedbackImmediate. Correct: green flash + ✓. Incorrect: shake + ✗ + explanation.

Exercise Types

MCQ

Multiple Choice

Question with 3-4 options. Tap to select. Immediate color feedback: correct option turns green, incorrect turns red with brief shake animation. Only one attempt allowed.

  1. Question text displayed
  2. User taps one option → option highlights
  3. Auto-check: correct (green + ✓) or incorrect (red + ✗ + correct answer revealed)
  4. Brief explanation shown below if incorrect
  5. User scrolls to continue
Fill Blank

Fill in the Blank

Sentence with a blank. Either a dropdown (if options provided) or a text input with Tamil IME. Check button validates against correct answer.

  1. Sentence with inline blank (dropdown or input)
  2. If input: Tamil IME activates with transliteration suggestions
  3. User taps "Check" → correct (green) or incorrect (red + "Try again")
  4. Retry allowed for fill-in-blank (up to 3 attempts)
  5. After 3 failed attempts, correct answer revealed
Short

Short Answer

Open-ended question with a textarea. Tamil IME active. No auto-grading — user self-checks against model answer.

  1. Question displayed
  2. User writes answer in textarea (Tamil IME available)
  3. User taps "Show model answer" to reveal
  4. Model answer appears below in green box
  5. User self-assesses and continues
Long

Long Answer

Essay-style question. Larger textarea with word count hint. Same reveal pattern as short answer. Used for deeper analysis and reflection.

  1. Question with optional word count minimum
  2. User writes in large textarea
  3. Word count shown live (if minimum specified)
  4. "Show model answer" reveals reference answer
Key principle: Exercises should never feel like tests. They're more like "pause and think about what you just read." The gold background makes them visually distinct from reading content but not jarring. The learner can scroll past without answering if they want — but unanswered exercises are tracked and shown in the post-lesson summary.

Section 15

Interaction Patterns

Seven core interactions that the learner performs repeatedly. Each must be polished to feel instant and satisfying.

Word tap → Definition

1. Click-to-Lookup

  1. User taps a Tamil word in the text
  2. Word gets subtle yellow highlight (immediately, before API responds)
  3. Dictionary panel appears/updates: skeleton state while loading
  4. Definition loads (target: <300ms). Panel populated.
  5. Tapping another word replaces the current lookup
  6. Tapping elsewhere or pressing Esc dismisses
Answer → Feedback

2. Exercise Submit

  1. User selects/types answer
  2. MCQ: auto-submits on selection. Fill-in: "Check" button.
  3. Correct: green flash animation, ✓ icon, 200ms delay, then proceed
  4. Incorrect: red flash + shake (300ms), ✗ icon, correct answer shown
  5. Exercise card collapses slightly after completion (reduces visual weight)
Tap → Flip → Rate

3. Flashcard Flip

  1. Card front: shows Tamil word centered
  2. Tap or press Space: 3D Y-axis rotation (300ms)
  3. Card back: definition, example, source lesson
  4. Rating buttons appear below (slide up, 200ms delay)
  5. Tap rating or press 1-4: card slides out left, next card slides in from right
Scroll → Progress

4. Reading Progress

  1. Progress bar at top of reader fills as user scrolls through content
  2. TOC dots in left margin fill green as each section is scrolled past
  3. Active section name highlighted in TOC
  4. Scroll position saved to localStorage on debounce (500ms)
  5. On lesson reopen: auto-scroll to saved position with toast notification
Type → Tamil

5. Tamil IME Input

  1. User focuses a text input in an exercise
  2. Types English characters (e.g., "tha")
  3. Transliteration suggestions appear in horizontal strip (above keyboard on mobile)
  4. Tap suggestion or press number key (1-5) to insert Tamil text
  5. Space key commits the first suggestion and adds a space
  6. Escape or typing continues dismisses suggestions
TOC → Jump

  1. User clicks a section name in the left TOC margin
  2. Reading surface smooth-scrolls to that section (400ms ease-out)
  3. Target section briefly pulses (subtle background flash)
  4. TOC updates to show new active section
  5. Progress bar and dot colors update accordingly

  2. Button transitions: text changes to "✓ Saved", color turns green (300ms)

Section 16

States & Edge Cases

Every screen has at least four states. The app should handle each gracefully, never showing a blank screen or an uncaught error.

Global States

StateTriggerHandling
Offline Network lost mid-session Toast: "You're offline. Changes will sync when you reconnect." Reading continues from cache. Dictionary lookups show "Offline — definition unavailable." Exercises work if already loaded. Flashcard ratings queued and synced on reconnect.
Slow network API responses > 2s Skeleton loaders everywhere. Dictionary shows "Looking up…" with spinning indicator. Never show blank space waiting for content.
Session expired Auth token expired Silent refresh attempt. If fails: gentle redirect to landing page. "Your session expired. Please sign in again." No data loss — progress saved incrementally.
Lesson not found Invalid lesson ID in URL Friendly 404: "This lesson doesn't exist." Back to lesson browser button.
First-time (empty) New user, no progress data Home shows "Start your first lesson" instead of "Continue." All stat cards show encouraging copy instead of zeros.
All complete Every lesson finished Home shows celebration card. Continue card becomes "Review" suggesting flashcards or re-reading. Lesson browser shows all cards with green checkmarks.

Edge Cases

CaseHandling
Very long Tamil word overflows container CSS word-break: break-word on all Tamil text containers. Dictionary panel and flashcard cards use flexible height.
Dictionary returns no result for a word "No definition found for [word]." No save-to-flashcards button. Suggest alternative spellings if available.
User navigates away mid-exercise Exercise state saved to localStorage per lesson. On return, answers are restored. Unanswered exercises remain unanswered.
User closes app mid-lesson Scroll position saved every 500ms (debounced). On next visit, resume from saved position.
Flashcard queue is empty "You're all caught up!" with illustration. Show next review time. "Back to reading" CTA.
Very short lesson (1-2 sections) Layout still works — TOC is minimal, exercises may not appear. Post-lesson summary still shows stats.
Very long lesson (20+ sections) TOC scrolls independently. Progress bar is accurate. Performance: sections lazy-load as user scrolls near them.

Section 20

Implementation Priority

Ordered by impact on the learning experience. Each phase is independently shippable.

1

Interleaved Exercises in Reader

Move exercises from the right sidebar into the content scroll flow. This is the single highest-impact change — it transforms passive reading into active learning. Requires refactoring LessonReader content ordering and removing exercises from DictionaryPanel.

2

Simplified Home Screen

Replace the current dashboard (sidebar, calendar, lesson grid) with the single-purpose resume screen. One continue card + three stat cards. Remove the sidebar. This eliminates 70% of visual noise and gets users reading faster.

3

Dictionary Panel → Dictionary Only

Refactor DictionaryPanel to be purely dictionary (no exercises). Add "Save to flashcards" button. Add "recently looked up" word chips. Desktop: right margin. Mobile: bottom sheet (new BottomSheet component).

4

Post-Lesson Summary Screen

New route /lesson/[id]/complete. Shows stats, vocabulary recap, CTAs for flashcard review and next lesson. Bridges lesson completion to vocabulary retention.

5

Reading Progress Persistence

Save scroll position to localStorage per lesson. Auto-resume on revisit. Toast notification "Resuming where you left off." Exercise answer state also persisted.

6

Flashcard Enhancements

Keyboard shortcuts (Space, 1-4). Source lesson shown on card back. Lesson filter. Session complete screen with breakdown. Next review intervals on rating buttons.

7

Lesson Browser Redesign

Replace LessonList with new LessonBrowser. Status filters (All/In Progress/Completed). Cleaner cards with progress bars. Separate route /lessons.

8

Onboarding Flow

3-step onboarding for new users. Tamil level, daily goal, interests. Personalized first lesson recommendation.

9

Progress Dashboard

New /progress route with curriculum progress, streak calendar, vocabulary stats, time tracking, accuracy trends.

10

TOC Redesign (Dot-Line Nav)

Replace the current 350px TOC sidebar with a minimal 200px margin using dot/line progress indicator. Dots fill as sections are completed.

Phasing: Items 1-3 form Phase 1 (core learning loop). Items 4-6 form Phase 2 (retention loop). Items 7-10 form Phase 3 (polish and expansion). Each phase can be shipped independently.
